What we do?

Life Change Ministry (LCM) is a registered NPO (090-057) which aims to give life and hope. Whilst engaging with the drug abuse problem in Wynberg in the last year, LCM has observed that there are approximately 200 Homeless in the area, of which 60-70% are struggling with a drug addiction and spending about R1,500-R3,500pm on alcohol and illicit drugs due to the loss of a loved one, a job, broken family (alcohol, drugs, separation or abuse), bad friendships and a general lack of self-esteem.

Why we do it?

The social inequality regarding homeless in South Africa motivated us (a group of postgraduates and UCT Alumni) to invest all our human, material and financial resources to address the issue by creating a Non Profit Organisation called â€œLife Change Ministry" in 2009. LCM was providing services such as feeding programmes, CV writing, and job hunting. From the start LCM was helping around 4-5 people per week with the above mentioned services and hosting monthly empowerment meetings with speakers from different levels of society with around 8-10 impoverished attendees of all ages. In 2010 LCM helped many people find jobs, facilitated funding for training programmes, foods, clothes and accommodation.
